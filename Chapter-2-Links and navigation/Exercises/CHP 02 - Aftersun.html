aftersun

Overview

Sophie reflects on the shared joy and private melancholy of a holiday she took with her father twenty years earlier. Memories real and imagined fill the gaps between miniDV footage as she tries to reconcile the father she knew with the man she didn’t.

CAST and CHARACTERS

01. Sophie (Frankie Corio)

Sophie is an 11-year-old girl who spends precious time with her loving and idealistic father, Calum, during a fading vacation at a Turkish resort in the late 1990s. As adolescence begins to unfold, Sophie witnesses her father’s struggles beyond the idyllic moments they share.

02. Calum (Paul Mescal)

Calum grapples with the weight of life outside of fatherhood while creating cherished memories with Sophie. Calum’s love for his daughter speaks volumes about him, even though his fate is marked by tragedy. The film subtly implies that he either commits suicide or faces a heartbreaking end due to self-destructive behavior or mental health issues.

REVIEWS

01. Rotten Tomatoes

The film has garnered 96% on the Tomatometer, with 243 reviews. Critics praise the movie, particularly Frankie Corio’s tremendous performance.

02. The Guardian

This effortlessly relaxed debut by Charlotte Wells is a subtle and complex exploration of post-divorce parenthood. It’s a sunny delight that beautifully captures the dynamics between a father (played by Paul Mescal) and his daughter.

03. Time Out

“Aftersun” flows like a fondly remembered memory, replayed endlessly in search of crucial details. Wells’s direction sets an easy pace, bringing out the best in the central performers.
