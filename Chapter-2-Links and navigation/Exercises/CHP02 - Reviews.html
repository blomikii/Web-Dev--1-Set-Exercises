01. Rotten Tomatoes

The film has garnered 96% on the Tomatometer, with 243 reviews. Critics praise the movie, particularly Frankie Corio’s tremendous performance.

02. The Guardian

This effortlessly relaxed debut by Charlotte Wells is a subtle and complex exploration of post-divorce parenthood. It’s a sunny delight that beautifully captures the dynamics between a father (played by Paul Mescal) and his daughter.

03. Time Out

“Aftersun” flows like a fondly remembered memory, replayed endlessly in search of crucial details. Wells’s direction sets an easy pace, bringing out the best in the central performers.
